# OpenConstruction Governance

OpenConstruction is governed as a **distributed, community-driven open-source ecosystem**.
Authority and responsibility are role-based, transparent, and designed to evolve
with the community.


## Governance Principles

- Openness and transparency
- Distributed responsibility
- Merit-based advancement
- Rotating leadership and stewardship
- Sustainability beyond any single institution or individual


## Roles & Responsibilities

### Core Maintainers
- Provide platform-wide stewardship
- Oversee governance processes and standards
- Resolve conflicts and coordinate major decisions

### Module Maintainers
- Maintain specific catalogs or platform modules
- Review related pull requests
- Mentor contributors within their scope

### Stewards & Reviewers
- Conduct quality reviews
- Support schema compliance and best practices
- Assist with contributor onboarding

### Release Leads
- Coordinate periodic releases
- Manage changelogs and versioning
- Ensure CI/CD readiness for release

### Community Contributors
- Propose and review contributions
- Participate in discussions and roadmap planning
- May be nominated into stewardship roles over time


## Decision-Making

- Technical and roadmap proposals are discussed publicly
- Consensus is preferred; maintainers facilitate resolution
- Significant changes are documented and versioned


## Role Rotation & Succession

All leadership roles are:
- Open to the community
- Rotating and time-bound
- Based on contribution history and community trust


## Amendments

Governance policies may be updated through community discussion and
maintainer consensus, with changes documented publicly.
